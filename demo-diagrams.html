The Enclosure Revolution

How zoo science solved the problem human civilisation hasn't.

1907
Hagenbeck opens Tierpark Hamburg. Moats instead of bars. The animal is considered for the first time. Treated as architectural novelty.
1950s
Hediger at Zürich Zoo: animals aren't "wild animals in cages" — they're organisms responding to environmental pressures. Design the environment around the biology.
1965
The default: concrete pen, iron bars. Designed for the keeper's convenience. The animal is visible, contained, and miserable.
1979
Five Freedoms codified. Freedom from hunger, discomfort, pain, fear, and inability to express normal behaviour. A minimum standard. Necessary but not sufficient.
1994
Mellor introduces Five Domains at Massey University. The shift: welfare is not the absence of harm but the presence of positive states. Curiosity. Play. Agency. Comfort.
2026
For Homo sapiens? Still working on it. This book is an attempt.
